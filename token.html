Token utility

MIMO serves four core functions within the protocol:

1. Governance

MIMO holders vote on protocol parameters: supported chains, fee structures, solver eligibility, and treasury allocation. Voting power scales with lock duration (veToken model).

2. Solver staking

Solvers must stake MIMO to participate in the yield routing network. Stake size determines bid priority. Malicious behavior results in slashing.

3. Fee sharing

30% of all protocol fees are distributed to MIMO stakers. Remaining 70% goes to treasury, solver rewards, and liquidity incentives.

4. Access control

certain premium features (priority routing, higher deposit limits, advanced strategies) require minimum MIMO holdings.
